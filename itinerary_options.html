March trip

Start: Zagreb (Mon 16 Mar) — Return to Munich by Sun 22 Mar • Van sleeping

Introduction — what to expect

This seven‑day loop blends turquoise lakes, dramatic karst canyons, salty Adriatic sunsets and a final alpine breather.

Plitvice Lakes' tufa waterfalls, the ancient waterfront of Zadar and its Sea Organ, the climbing gorges of Paklenica, the stepped falls of Krka, the picture‑postcard stops of Rastoke and Karlovac, then over the border to Ljubljana and tranquil Lake Bled (with an optional Hallstatt detour before Munich). Expect varied scenery every day — from forested boardwalks to coastal promenades and limestone cliffs.

Logistics at a glance: average daily driving is moderate — most legs are around 1.5–3 hours, with one longer transfer day (~4–6 hours) on the return toward Munich. March is shoulder season: coastal days typically sit around 8–15°C, inland and alpine mornings/evenings can be near freezing (0–5°C). Bring layered clothing, a waterproof wind‑layer and footwear suitable for damp boardwalks and rocky trails.

Budget snapshot: Total up to €1,000 (≈€500 each). Typical mid‑estimate ~€820 — rough breakdown: fuel ~€230, tolls/vignettes ~€45, food ~€280, activities ~€210 (campsites and extras included in totals).

Why this route: it keeps driving efficient while prioritising experiences — early mornings at Plitvice for quiet photos, seaside sunsets in Zadar, technical or beginner‑friendly climbs in Paklenica, and easy waterfall walks at Krka. The Slovenia leg slows the pace with market strolls and alpine vistas before finishing in Munich.

Day 1 — Mon 16 Mar: Zagreb → Plitvice Lakes NP

~130 km • ~2.0–2.5 h drive
Image source: Minority Nomad
  • Season & timing: March 16 falls in shoulder/low season — far fewer visitors than summer, so you can relax a bit. That said, tour groups often start arriving around 10:00, so the best photos and quieter paths are from opening until ~09:30. (source: Minority Nomad)
  • Where to start: Entrance 1 covers the Lower Lakes (Veliki Slap / Skradinski Buk viewpoints). Entrance 2 gives quicker access to the Upper Lakes and can feel quieter; many guides recommend Entrance 2 if you want lighter crowds coming from Zagreb. Choose based on which trails you pick.
  • Hiking routes (approx):
    • Trail A — ~3.5 km, 2–3 h (short, Lower Lakes; great if you want a shorter morning visit).
    • Trail B — ~4.0 km, 3–4 h (covers most Lower Lakes; includes boat + train segments).
    • Trail C — ~8.0 km, 4–5 h (longer loop covering Lower + Upper Lakes; scenic and quieter in upper parts).
    • Trail E — ~5.5 km, 2–3 h (Upper Lakes circuit with waterfalls; good half‑day option).
    • Trail K / full loop — up to ~15–18 km (full‑day hike; adventurous option if you want to explore most of the park on foot).
  • Maps & where to download: official park map (JPG/PDF) is available on the park site: Plitvice Lakes — Park Map (direct image: Karta-NPPJ_2022.jpg). Visitor-friendly trail maps and route pages with distances are on Plitvice‑Lakes.info (trails A/B/C/E).
  • Ticket price: 10 Euro for adults in March (official rates can change; verify on the park ticketing page).
  • Opening times in March: 8:00 to 16:00 (check the park site for any changes or temporary closures).
  • Practical tips: buy tickets online (official ticketing: ticketing.np-plitvicka-jezera.hr) or check availability; check the park Current Conditions & Alerts before arrival (some routes or boat/train services close in heavy rain/snow).
  • Gear & food: grippy shoes (boardwalks can be wet/icy), waterproof layer, small first‑aid, water and picnic food (park cafés are limited and spread out), extra batteries/memory for photos.
  • Transport inside park: boats and panoramic trains/buses operate seasonally — they shorten walking but check schedules; walking the whole park is possible if boats/trains are not running.
  • Safety & etiquette: stay on marked boardwalks (tufa barriers are fragile), don't swim, and be respectful—some narrow sections can bottleneck when crowded; be patient and aim for early start to avoid crowds.

Day 2 — Tue 17 Mar: Plitvice → Zadar

~125 km • ~1.5–2.0 h drive
Image source: Wikimedia Commons (Zadar)
  • Morning: optional sunrise/extra hour at Plitvice, then drive to Zadar and aim to arrive before noon to explore.
  • Must-sees: Sea Organ (unique coastal instrument), Greeting to the Sun installation, Roman forum and St. Donatus Church in the old town.
  • Active add-on: rent a bike and cycle the coastal route west toward Nin for salt flats and quiet beaches (~20–30km round trip).
  • Timing: allow 2–3 hours for old town walking and seaside installations; cafes around the harbor serve cheap fish sandwiches.
  • Practical: paid parking near the harbor or camper parking outside town; campsites have showers (recommended in March).

Zadar is compact, atmospheric and full of small surprises — ancient Roman ruins meet bold contemporary art on the waterfront. Plan to arrive by late morning so you can wander the old town, time the sunset at the promenade, and still have energy for a short active option.

Must‑see highlights (short descriptions)

  • Sea Organ: an experimental set of submerged pipes that play tones as the sea moves — best experienced seated on the steps at high or rising tide (10–20 minutes).
  • Greeting to the Sun: a glass disc installation that stores solar energy and produces a light show after dusk — pair this with Sea Organ for a memorable sunset/sundown visit (15–20 minutes).
  • St. Donatus & Roman Forum: 9th‑century circular church overlooking well-preserved Roman forum ruins; vivid place for photos and a short history stop (20–40 minutes).
  • Five Wells Square & Kalelarga: relaxed cafés and a good place to rest; Kalelarga (main street) runs through the old town with bakeries and small shops.
  • Nin & salt pans (optional): a short drive (20–30 min) to the medieval town of Nin with shallow sandy beaches and traditional saltworks — great for birdwatching and easy cycling.

Suggested mini‑itineraries (pick one)

  • Quick cultural (2–3h): Roman Forum → St. Donatus → Five Wells Square → Sea Organ → Greeting to the Sun at sunset.
  • Active half‑day (3–4h): Rent bikes or e-bikes; ride west to Nin (20–30km round trip depending on route), explore salt pans and return for sunset.
  • Relaxed day (all day): Old town cafés, harbour fish sandwich lunch, stroll to marina, optional short boat cruise along the coast (seasonal).

Activities & where to book

  • Kayak / SUP: rentals operate from the waterfront in-season; in March check provider openings but local shops often run off-season by appointment. Search "Zadar kayak rental" for providers.
  • Boat trips: Kornati day trips and island cruises run from Zadar in warmer months — in March services are limited but private charters may be available; book locally if needed.
  • Bike rental: many shops near the old town rent city or e-bikes; use them to reach Nin, the salt pans and quieter beaches.

Food & budget tips

  • Harbour fish sandwich (riblji sendvič) stalls near the Riva: cheap, filling and a local staple.
  • Market & bakeries: pick up burek or pastries for breakfast; Pazar (Green Market) has produce and cheeses for a picnic.
  • Konoba dining: avoid the most central terraces if on a budget — walk a street or two inland for better prices and portions.

Logistics & practical tips

  • Timing: plan to be at the Sea Organ/Greeting to the Sun 30–60 minutes before sunset. Local sunsets are often cited among Croatia's best — Hitchcock famously liked Zadar's sunset.
  • Parking: paid parking near the harbor; camper parking and campsites are a short drive out (Park4Night listings recommended).
  • Weather & seasonality: March is shoulder season — fewer crowds, but some rentals and boat services may be limited. Call ahead for kayak/boat providers if you need to guarantee availability.
  • Safety: the Sea Organ steps are wet and can be slippery; watch for uneven pavement in the old town and bring layers for cool sea breezes at sunset.

Day 3 — Wed 18 Mar: Zadar → Paklenica NP (Starigrad Paklenica)

~35 km • ~40–50 min
Image source: Total Croatia News

Paklenica is a striking contrast to the lakes — steep karst canyons, sheer limestone walls and a strong climbing tradition. It’s excellent for a day of adrenaline or a relaxed canyon hike.

Highlights & context

  • Anića Kuk: the most famous climbing wall — compact sport routes and long multi‑pitch lines attract climbers from Europe. The wall towers above the trail and is photogenic.
  • Velika & Mala Paklenica canyons: deep gorges carved into Dinaric limestone with waterfalls, caves and endemic flora — a great place for canyon walks and wildlife spotting.

Suggested activities

  • Climbing: day routes for intermediate climbers; beginners can book a half‑day guided intro or top‑rope session in Starigrad. Search "Paklenica climbing guide" and compare local guide reviews.
  • Hiking: Velika Paklenica trail to the canyon head (2–4 h roundtrip) or a gentler walk to the river for photography (1–2 h).
  • Via ferrata & canyoning: limited options — check local outfitters; some sections can be technical and weather dependent in March.

Practical & safety tips

  • Arrive early to secure parking and enjoy cooler canyon temperatures; the canyon can be shaded and chilly even in March.
  • Bring helmet and appropriate shoes for climbing; if renting gear or hiring guides, confirm availability a day before (Starigrad providers).
  • Water & snacks: there are few facilities inside the park — stock up in Starigrad or Zadar beforehand.
  • Roads: winding coastal roads — allow extra 20–30 minutes for transfers if you stop for viewpoints.

Day 4 — Thu 19 Mar: Paklenica → Krka (Skradin) / Šibenik

~100 km to Skradin • ~1.5 h
Image source: Grey Line

Krka National Park is famous for accessible waterfalls and easy boardwalk walks — a calmer, splashy counterpoint to canyon hiking.

Highlights & context

  • Skradinski buk: a wide, stepped waterfall system with wooden boardwalks and viewpoints — very photogenic and family‑friendly.
  • Roški slap and river canyons: upstream areas worth visiting if you want quieter scenery and short hikes away from the main falls.

Suggested activities

  • Boardwalk walk: Skradinski buk circuit 1–2 h, easy walking and multiple viewpoints — ideal for a relaxed morning.
  • Boat shuttle: seasonal boats link Skradin and some park piers — useful if you want a shorter walking day or boat photos.
  • Kayak/river swim: swimming rules vary by season — in March it's often cool and some areas restricted; guided kayak trips run in summer mainly, check seasonality.

Practical tips

  • Buy park tickets in advance where possible and check opening hours for March; Skradin has parking and harbour facilities.
  • Bring waterproof layers — mist from the falls can make paths damp; boardwalks can be slippery in early spring.
  • Šibenik evening: stroll to St. James Cathedral and the harbour; small konobas offer budget plates — try grilled fish or lokal šopska salad.

Day 5 — Fri 20 Mar: Šibenik → Karlovac / Rastoke → Zagreb

~220 km total with stops • ~2.5–3.5 h driving depending on stops
Image source: Intrepid Travel

This travel day is purposely lighter — scenic short stops break the drive and showcase lesser-known Lika region charms.

Highlights & suggested stops

  • Rastoke (Slunj): small waterfalls and traditional watermills at the river confluence — 30–60 minute walk with great photo opportunities.
  • Karlovac: a convenient larger town for bakery breaks, fuel and a park stroll; good place to stock up before Zagreb.

Logistics & tips

  • Plan 30–60 minute stops at Rastoke; parking is available but can be limited—arrive mid-morning or mid-afternoon to avoid peak local times.
  • Zagreb overnight: recommended campsite or paid camper parking with shower access; supermarkets and bakeries open earlier than other services.

Day 6 — Sat 21 Mar: Zagreb → Ljubljana → Bled (Slovenia)

~380 km total (Zagreb → Ljubljana ~1.5h; Ljubljana → Bled ~45min)
Image source: Wikimedia Commons

This day balances a touch of vibrant Ljubljana city life with alpine calm at Lake Bled — a great way to reset before the long drive home.

Ljubljana — quick highlights

  • Old Town & Dragon Bridge: compact pedestrian centre with cafés and markets — perfect for a 1–2 hour stroll and breakfast.
  • Central Market: local produce, cheeses and bakery treats — good spot to pick picnic supplies for Bled.

Lake Bled — suggested activities

  • Lakeshore walk: ~6 km loop takes ~1.5–2 h at a relaxed pace; Ojstrica and Mala Osojnica viewpoints give classic photo angles.
  • Rowboat to Bled Island: seasonal but often available; traditional pletna boats operate in warmer months — check March availability.
  • Short hikes: Mala Osojnica (~30–45 min) for sunset, or walk to the castle (Bled Castle) for panoramas and museum exhibits.

Practical tips

  • Car parking: paid lots in both Ljubljana and Bled; allow time to find spots in peak tourist areas.
  • Food: try kremšnita pastry lakeside, or pack market picnic from Ljubljana Central Market for cheaper options.
  • Timing: afternoon at Bled is ideal for soft light; expect cooler alpine breezes in the evening.

Day 7 — Sun 22 Mar: Bled → Villach → Salzburg → Munich (finish)

~420–520 km depending on exact route • ~5–6 h drive
Image source: Wikimedia Commons (Hallstatt)

Final leg: a mostly motorway day with optional scenic detours — plan fuel, vignette purchase and rest stops to reach Munich comfortably by evening.

Route tips & optional stops

  • Villach: convenient for fuel and quick breaks after crossing from Slovenia.
  • Hallstatt (optional): beautiful lakeside village; allow 60–90 minutes for a viewpoint photo but expect narrow roads and parking delays.
  • Salzburg stop: if time allows, a short stroll near the fortress gives a scenic coffee stop before the final drive to Munich.

Practicalities

  • Buy Austrian vignette at border petrol stations (required for motorways) or online in advance.
  • Allow buffer time for border checks and traffic near Salzburg/Munich — start early and plan 2–3 short coffee/fuel stops.

Drive legs summary (approx)

  • Zagreb → Plitvice: ~130 km — 2.0–2.5 h
  • Plitvice → Zadar: ~125 km — 1.5–2.0 h
  • Zadar → Paklenica: ~35 km — 40–50 min
  • Paklenica → Skradin (Krka): ~100 km — 1.5 h
  • Krka/Šibenik → Zagreb: ~220 km — 2.5–3.0 h
  • Zagreb → Ljubljana: ~140 km — 1.5–2.0 h
  • Ljubljana → Bled: ~55 km — 45–60 min
  • Bled → Salzburg/Villach → Munich: ~400–500 km — 4–6 h (depending on stops and route)

Activity & logistics notes

  • Plitvice: no swimming; buy tickets online if busy; wear grippy shoes for wet boardwalks.
  • Paklenica: shade keeps rock cold/wet — helmet recommended; hire guide if missing gear.
  • Krka: boat shuttles vary by season — verify schedules; swimming rules change by month.
  • Zadar/Split parking: use paid lots or camper sites for safer overnight and shower access.

Budget food & markets (quick)

  • Plitvice area: pack supermarket picnic — park shops are pricier.
  • Zadar: harbour fish sandwiches and local bakeries; Pazar market for supplies.
  • Starigrad Paklenica: konobas with set plates (dnevni menu) — good value.
  • Split: Green Market for fruit/cheese; bakeries for cheap breakfasts.

Van & overnight tips

  • Prefer campsites or marinas for shower access; Park4Night helps find legal spots.
  • Carry a 20–30L water jerry, portable stove, powerbank, and spare fuel plan for long coastal legs.
  • Ventilate overnight to reduce condensation; use warm synthetic sleeping bags for damp nights.
  • Security: park in lit/populated areas, lock valuables, and use campsite facilities where possible.

Safety & weather

  • March weather: inland cool (near freezing nights), coast milder but windy. Expect 5–15°C daytime.
  • Check local forecasts before water activities or technical climbs; adapt plans if necessary.

Quick booking & search tips

  • Krka shuttle: search "Skradin Krka boat" for schedules.
